In my Heat Transfer course, one early assignment problem that I found particularly useful is the one shown below. The core concepts it focused on were surface energy conservation and  conduction and convection heat transfer modes. 

Beyond building my fundamental knowledge, I thought this problem was helpful because it was a realistic model of a thin film heater supplying heat to a plastic layer exposed to the environment, which occurs in many real life applications such as defrosting windshields, sensors, and refrigerated surfaces. In these cases, concerns of overheating and ideal operating conditions are very relevant, so it is interesting to think about the practical implications of applying different strategies to operate the heater under higher heat flux, as they may be more or less feasible depending on the application.
